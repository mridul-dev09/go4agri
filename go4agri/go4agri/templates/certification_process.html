Our Certification Lifecycle

At GO4AGRI, certification is not just a one-time approval — it is a continuous cycle of assessment, verification, and improvement. Our process ensures compliance, builds trust, and supports your commitment to organic integrity from application to renewal.

Organic Certification Lifecycle Details

1. Inquiry & Application

Applicant submits the initial application form along with general basic information about the entity.

2. Contract & Quotation

We provide a service quotation and a formal contract, which the applicant signs to finalize the agreement.

3. Document Review

Thorough review of the Organic System Plan (OSP/OMP), standard documentation, and supporting records.

4. Inspection Planning

An inspection plan is prepared and coordinated based on initial risk assessment and category parameters.

5. On-Site Inspection

Detailed audit of operations, physical inspection of facilities, fields, processes, and verification of records.

6. Sampling & Testing

Collection and testing of product, soil, or water samples by Go4agri Auditors and/or an accredited labs (as required or risk-based)..

7. NC / CAPA Follow Up

Any identified non-conformities are addressed, and corrective action evidence is submitted for review.

8. Technical Review

The inspection file, findings, and corrective actions are thoroughly reviewed by technical experts.

9. Certification Decision

Impartial certification decision is officially taken by an authorized, competent certification body panel.

10. Certificate Issued

The formal scope certificate is issued, and the operator details are listed in the official directory.

11. Surveillance & Monitoring

Regular announced/unannounced surveillance inspections, sampling, and record audits ensure ongoing compliance.

12. Renewal / Re-certification

The cycle continues annually with renewal applications submitted prior to certificate expiration.

Continuous Verification & Integrity Controls

To maintain strict organic integrity, our process is supported by unannounced inspections, risk-based verification, product sampling, investigation audits, complaint investigations, traceability & mass balance checks, market surveillance, and suspension/sanction controls.
